Music

Piano

I have played piano since elementary school and, I am level 10 in the Music Teacher's Association of California's Certificate of Merit Program. I was also a CM Panel finalist and invited to play in the annual MTAC State Convention Recitals.

Gershwin Prelude No. 3

Although the shortest piece in my Certificate of Merit Panel Audition repertoire, Gershwin's third Prelude was perhaps the most challenging of the five pieces, because of its relatively fast tempo and contemporary style.

Schubert Impromptu Op.90 No.4

As for the Romantic style, Schubert Impromptu Op.90 No.4 is one of the composer's most popular impromptu's for its exercise on tone quality and dynamic range.

Bach Fugue No.2 in C Minor

Bach's pieces are regarded as one of the most healthy ways to train hand dexterity and independance. As my teacher tells me, Bach is about making it look easy.

Mozart Piano Sonata No. 5 in G Major

Mozart Sonatas are like vanilla ice cream: soft, sweet, and nothing like vanilla. One must channel their inner snobbish, rich nobility to capture the essence of Mozart's music.

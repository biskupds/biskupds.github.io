Engineering

I am a junior studying Mechanical Engineering at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and my passion is making useful, easy to use creations.

Eco Illini Supermileage: G5e Battery Pack

In fall 2021 I am creating a brand new battery pack for the G5e prototype vehicle of Eco Illini Supermileage, a Registered Student Organization at UIUC. Each year the club competes in the Shell Eco Marathon to achieve the most efficient prototype car.

The updated pack (right) is visibly smaller than the previous generation (left), yet exhibits 25% less weight despite its 33.3% greater volumetric energy density.

The pack consists of 13 Samsung 40T 21700 lithium-ion cells in series achieving 187.2 Watt-hours of energy. The cells are packed hexagonally into a 3D-printed casing which was modeled using Fusion360 and printed in PLA.

Lucid Motors: Busbar Joint Resistance Heating

While interning with Lucid Motors, I investigated how the mechanical design of battery pack busbars can be thermally optimized.

Temperature Approximation from Current Profile

I derived an equation relating busbar temperature and time under electrical current and used this to investigate a pracical upper-bound on busbar joint resistance. The system ignores conductive heat transfer and only considers convective heat loss, thus the predicted temperatures will be higher than observed.

Using numerical integration, a table of current vs time can be used to evaluate the change in busbar temperature associated with each time step. When applied to a known initial temperature, a plot of the busbar's temperature vs time for a given current profile can be produced.

Range and Horsepower Losses from Joint Resistance

I needed to investigate the extent to which the Lucid Air's performance would suffer if busbar joint resistances changed.

Once again using numerical integration, it was calculated that (at the estimated efficiency) if each high voltage joint in the pack had an increase in resistance of 30μΩ, the entire pack would exhibit a decrease in range of 0.00297mi/8mi = 0.033%.

As for peak horsepower, the loss in power from the increase in resistance can be calculated from an assumed max current of 1200 Amps. The power dissipation is simply the square of the peak current multiplied by the increase in resistance, which totaled to 3.682 horsepower. It was evident from this analysis that most of the resistance of the pack comes from the battery cells themselves, and that most high voltage busbars in the pack are already quite robust from a performance standpoint.

Art

Chinese Calligraphy

I began taking private Chinese Calligraphy lessons in the sixth grade under a family friend. I started off with the print script (正楷) font to master the fundamentals, and then later learned to express myself in cursive (行書) and artistic/imaginative (草書) character styles. Here is some of my artwork below.

下江陵 Xia Jiangling - 李白 Li Bai

This iconic poem by Li Bai portrays his observations of nature on his way down the Yangtze River to Baidi City. Li Bai experiences the hot and noisy jungle environment, which is reflected in the faster brushstrokes of my cursive style piece.

On the left is my rendition of the poem in print script. Written when I was in junior high, the piece demonstrates my technical skill with the brush and my fundamental knowledge of the different types of calligraphy strokes.

On the right is the same poem revisited with a cursive style. Written in high school, I wanted to add character and life to a piece I had written in monotone before.

立春偶書 Li Chun Ou Shu - 張拭 Zhang Shi

This poem by Zhang Shi describes the rebirth of life that results from the seasonal transition from winter to spring. I used a cursive style with more animated brush strokes to symbolize the sweet youth of spring, as if the brush was dancing energetically.

Paintings

Since the seventh grade, painting has been my way of training my intuitive sense of spacial awareness. I paint colorful scenery, architecture, animals, and humans. My teacher retired in my junior year of high school, and I continued on my own.

Horizon

I grew up watching television shows like NOVA on PBS and How the Universe Works on the Science Channel, and always wondered what sort of spacecraft it would take to for humanity to live comfortably far from planet Earth. Here is my prediction of what a space station in the distant future might look like.

Weeping Elephant

While humans may have a relatively easy time adjusting to climate change from air pollutants, other animals find it much more difficult. Little by little, the extinction of minor species ripple through the food chain, diminishing biodiversity and exposing other species to vulnerability. I intended to capture the image of a worried elephant, a symbol for all the victims of humanity’s greedy abuse of natural resources.

Young Newton

As an aspiring engineer, I owe Sir Isaac Newton immensely for his contributions to modern mechanics. This is my homage to Newton, depicting the famous moment when he was struck by a falling apple and hypothesized his theory of Gravity. People often comment that my portrayal of Newton in thought resembles my own thinking pose.

Backyard

After a particularly heavy overnight rain (for a Californian, of course), the backyard of my home was draped with a thin layer of water, giving the illusion that a river ran through our garden. Surprised by this variation on a familiar scene, and I tried reimagining my garden as a river environment.

Prosperity

I am astounded by the extent to which humanity has used fire to conquer the planet. Humans have conquered the lands and the oceans with combustion engines, which harnesses the firepower of rapid, controlled explosions. Most of all, humans have conquered the night with electric light bulbs, powered by mainly the combustion of fossil fuels. In this piece, I display the progress that humanity has made in developing as a civilization, the main source of light coming from man-made objects.

Mom and Dad

This painting was an anniversary gift for my parents, and it symbolizes the balance of our family on many different levels. The two fish resemble the traditional balance of Yin and Yang energies, but as a biracial person, they also symbolize my family’s multicultural composition with both American and Taiwanese influences.

Music

Piano

I dabbled with the piano by ear during elementary school, but began more serious music training later in junior high. The year before high school I picked up violin and joined the Homestead High School Orchestra the following year. I am level 10 in the Music Teacher's Association of California's Certificate of Merit for Piano Program and also a CM Panel finalist. I also played piano for the Homestead Symphony Orchestra in my Junior and Senior years of high school.

Gershwin Prelude No. 3

Although the shortest piece in my Certificate of Merit Panel Audition repertoire, Gershwin's third Prelude was perhaps the most challenging of the five pieces, because of its relatively fast tempo and contemporary style.

Schubert Impromptu Op.90 No.4

As for the Romantic style, Schubert Impromptu Op.90 No.4 is one of the composer's most popular impromptu's for its exercise on tone quality and dynamic range. Playing this piece requires even tone quality and precise fingers.

Bach Fugue No.2 in C Minor

Bach's pieces are regarded as one of the most healthy ways to train hand dexterity and independance. As my teacher tells me, Bach is about making it look easy even when it makes you feel like a beginner again.

Mozart Piano Sonata No. 5 in G Major

Mozart Sonatas are like vanilla ice cream: soft, sweet, and nothing like vanilla. One must channel their inner snobbish, rich nobility to capture the essence of Mozart's music.
